Experiments 4 A/B tests grounded in specific findings from Surya's data. Each one addresses a distinct coaching gap that would be validated at scale across WHOOP's member base.
Test 01 — Recovery Warning
Predictive vs Reactive Coaching
"Does sending a recovery warning the evening before a predicted bad day outperform the morning after?"
ControlWarning sent morning after resting HR spikes
TreatmentWarning sent evening before — predicted from respiratory rate rising
TriggerResp rate 15% above 7-day personal baseline
MeasureNext-night sleep score + resting HR
Data basis
Recovery warnings well-timed only 37% of the time
Test 02 — Activity Push
Threshold-Gated vs Always-On
"Do activity insights land better when only sent after 3 consecutive below-average days?"
ControlActivity push sent regardless of current step level
TreatmentPush only triggered after 3 days below 7-day personal average
Trigger3-day rolling step deficit vs personal baseline
MeasureSteps in 7 days following insight
Data basis
Steps dropped -737 after activity push — sent on already-active days
Test 03 — Sleep Warning
Respiratory Rate as Early Warning
"Does acting on respiratory rate before sleep score drops improve sleep outcomes?"
ControlSleep warning when sleep score drops below threshold
TreatmentWarning when resp rate rises above baseline — 1 night earlier
TriggerResp rate >19.4 breaths/min (bad-sleep threshold from data)
MeasureSleep score next 3 nights
Data basis
Bad sleep nights avg 19.4 vs good sleep nights 18.3 breaths/min
Test 04 — Positive Reinforcement
Intensity Coaching vs Volume Praise
"For members with stable workout count but declining intensity — does effort coaching outperform frequency praise?"
Control"Great job staying consistent!" — frequency focused
Treatment"Your body is ready to push harder today" — intensity focused
TriggerWorkout count stable, avg exercise HR declining 4+ weeks
MeasureAverage exercise HR next 4 weeks
